UPSC Prelims Examination (Screening Stage)

The Preliminary Examination consists of two objective-type (Multiple Choice Question) papers conducted on the same day. It is purely a qualifying stage, and the marks obtained are not added to the final merit list. However, clearing this stage is mandatory to write the Mains exam.

Paper I: General Studies (GS-I)

This paper is the basis for the Prelims cutoff. It carries 200 marks with 100 questions, and includes negative marking for incorrect answers. The syllabus is vast, covering:

  • Current events of national and international importance.
  • History of India and Indian National Movement.
  • Indian and World Geography (Physical, Social, Economic).
  • Indian Polity and Governance (Constitution, Political System, Panchayati Raj, Public Policy).
  • Economic and Social Development (Sustainable Development, Poverty, Demographics).
  • General issues on Environmental Ecology, Biodiversity, and Climate Change.
  • General Science.

A score above the announced cutoff in this paper, along with qualifying Paper II, secures a place for the Mains examination.

Paper II: Civil Services Aptitude Test (CSAT)

CSAT is a compulsory qualifying paper carrying 200 marks with 80 questions. Candidates must score a minimum of 33% (i.e., 66.66 marks) to pass this paper. Failure to qualify in CSAT results in the GS-I paper not being evaluated. The focus areas are:

  • Comprehension.
  • Interpersonal skills including communication skills.
  • Logical reasoning and analytical ability.
  • Decision-making and problem-solving.
  • General mental ability.
  • Basic numeracy and data interpretation.
