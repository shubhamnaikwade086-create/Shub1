UPSC Civil Services Exam Guide

Your complete, simple and attractive guide to India's premier examination for civil services.

What is the UPSC CSE?

The Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) conducts the Civil Services Examination (CSE) annually to select candidates for various Group A and Group B Civil Services of the Government of India, including the Indian Administrative Service (IAS), Indian Police Service (IPS), and Indian Foreign Service (IFS).

This rigorous three-stage process is considered one of the toughest examinations globally, testing a candidate's intellectual caliber, general awareness, and personality traits suitable for high administrative roles.

Three Stages of Examination

The examination is strategically divided into three distinct phases:

  • Stage 1: Civil Services Preliminary Examination (Prelims) — An objective-type screening test, designed to filter candidates for the Main examination. It is purely qualifying in nature; marks are not counted towards the final merit list.
  • Stage 2: Civil Services Main Examination (Mains) — A written descriptive examination comprising nine papers. The marks secured in the seven merit-ranking papers are crucial for the final selection.
  • Stage 3: Personality Test (Interview) — A structured interview conducted by a board to assess the candidate's personality, mental alertness, critical powers of assimilation, and leadership qualities.

Final Merit and Passing Criteria

Aspirants must clear each stage sequentially. The final merit ranking is determined solely by the total marks obtained in the Mains Examination (1750 marks) and the Personality Test (275 marks), totaling 2025 marks.

The Prelims cutoff fluctuates yearly based on the difficulty of the paper and the number of vacancies. Success requires a strategic approach combining deep subject knowledge and exam-specific skills.
